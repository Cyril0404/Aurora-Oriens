### Heritage 页面内容

**The Crafts**

**Gold Filigree · 金银错**
*700 years of wirework, thinner than a human hair*

Our gold filigree pieces begin with gold wire drawn down to 0.08mm — thinner than a strand of silk. Artisans then twist, curl, and solder each filament by hand, following patterns that date back to the Ming Dynasty. A single pair of earrings takes four hours to complete. The result is jewelry that looks like lace made from sunlight.

**Cloisonné Enamel · 烧蓝珐琅**
*The art of painting with fire*

Cloisonné begins with a copper base. Artisans then solder fine metal wires to form the design outlines — the "cloisons" that give the technique its name. Colored glass powder is applied within each compartment, and the piece is fired at over 800°C. The process is repeated up to twelve times until the enamel sits flush with the wire. We use traditional Chinese color palettes: celadon greens, cinnabar reds, and the deep blues of Ming porcelain.

**Jade Micro-Carving · 玉石微雕**
*From the jade capital of China*

Nanyang, in Henan Province, has been the center of Chinese jade carving for over 2,000 years. Our jade pieces are sourced from verified deposits and hand-carved by artisans who trained for a minimum of eight years. The jade we use is nephrite — durable, warm to the touch, and believed in Chinese tradition to offer protection to its wearer.
