Our Story

Where Heritage Meets Everyday Luxury

Aurora Oriens was born from a simple belief: the world's most intricate craftsmanship shouldn't live only in museums.

We partner directly with artisans across China — from the jade carvers of Nanyang to the cloisonné masters of Beijing — to bring heritage techniques into pieces you can actually wear. Not behind glass. Not saved for special occasions. But woven into your everyday.

The Story Behind the Sparkle

Every piece begins with a story

The lotus that has symbolized purity in Chinese culture for a thousand years. The dragon motifs worn by emperors who believed in the power of protection. The filigree patterns passed down through families of goldsmiths whose youngest apprentices are now in their seventies.

We don't mass-produce these designs. We adapt them — preserving the technique, updating the silhouette, and making them accessible to the modern woman who moves through the world with intention.

The most personal piece of jewelry is one that says exactly what you want it to say.

The Heritage

Three Centuries of Craftsmanship

Each technique has been passed down through generations of Chinese artisans — and brought to life in our collection.

Gold Filigree

金银错 · 700 years of wirework

Thinner than a human hair

Our gold filigree pieces begin with gold wire drawn down to 0.08mm. Artisans twist, curl, and solder each filament by hand, following patterns that date back to the Ming Dynasty. A single pair of earrings takes four hours to complete. The result is jewelry that looks like lace made from sunlight.

Cloisonné Enamel

烧蓝珐琅 · The art of painting with fire

Fired at over 800°C

Cloisonné begins with a copper base. Fine metal wires are soldered to form the design outlines — the "cloisons" that give the technique its name. Colored glass powder is applied within each compartment and fired. The process is repeated up to twelve times. We use traditional Chinese color palettes: celadon greens, cinnabar reds, and deep blues of Ming porcelain.

Jade Micro-Carving

玉石微雕 · From the jade capital of China

Over 2,000 years of tradition

Nanyang, in Henan Province, has been the center of Chinese jade carving for over 2,000 years. Our jade pieces are sourced from verified deposits and hand-carved by artisans who trained for a minimum of eight years. The jade we use is nephrite — durable, warm to the touch, and believed in Chinese tradition to offer protection to its wearer.
